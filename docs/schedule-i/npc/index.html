All 4 Suppliers Unlock Guide: Ingredients and Dead Drop Locations

Jun 9, 2026
Short answer: There are 4 named suppliers in Schedule 1: Albert Hoover (weed seeds), Shirley Watts (pseudo for meth), Fungal Phil (shroom supplies), and Salvador Moreno (coca seeds). Each is unlocked by reaching Friendly status with one of their connections, then using Dead Drop orders to build relationship. Dan's Hardware is a shop (not a supplier NPC) -- the actual NPC shopkeeper is Dan Samwell.

How to Unlock Each Supplier

Suppliers are unlocked by reaching Friendly status with one of their connections. You cannot unlock them just by finding their stash location.

Albert Hoover (Northtown):

  • Available from the start of the game
  • Find his stash behind Dan's Hardware (alleyway north of Sauerkraut Supreme Pizza)
  • Use Dead Drop ordering through your phone's Messages app
  • Sells all tiers of marijuana seeds: OG Kush, Green Crack, Sour Diesel, etc.

Shirley Watts (Westville):

  • Unlocked by befriending Elizabeth Homley or Kevin Oakley
  • Stash located on the west face of the Warehouse (south of the motel, past the bridge)
  • Sells Pseudo -- the key ingredient for methamphetamine production

Fungal Phil (Downtown):

  • Unlocked through the same connections as Shirley (Elizabeth Homley or Kevin Oakley)
  • Stash located behind the central Gas-Mart in Downtown
  • Sells shroom spores for mushroom-based production (added in recent update)

Salvador Moreno (Docks):

  • Requires higher rank connections to unlock
  • Stash located behind the church on the east side of the map
  • Sells Coca Plant Seeds -- essential for cocaine production

Dead Drop Orders Explained

Dead Drop orders are how you build relationship with suppliers. Open your Messages app, select the supplier, and place a Dead Drop order.

Order process:

  1. Select up to 10 items per order
  2. Delivery takes ~30 min per item ordered (e.g., 4 seeds = 2 hours)
  3. Supplier messages you with the stash location when ready
  4. Location is shown on your map and compass

Relationship System

Each supplier has a relationship bar. Dead Drop orders increase it. When it fills, you unlock perks:

Relationship LevelEffect
FriendlyUnlocks the supplier, can place Dead Drop orders
LoyalUnlocks Meet Up option -- meet supplier in person (4 possible locations), no order limit
Maxed OutUnlocks Delivery -- order directly to your location via Delivery App

4 Meet Up locations (randomly chosen):

  1. Under north end of overpass
  2. Under west bridge
  3. West construction site
  4. Next to Mayor's house (north Suburbia)

Note: Only Dead Drop orders increase relationship. Meeting and buying directly does NOT build relationship progress.

Debt System

When you place Dead Drop orders, you accrue debt with the supplier. If debt gets too high, your order limit decreases. Eventually the supplier stops filling orders until you pay.

  • Pay debt at the supplier's stash location
  • Insert cash into the stash hatch
  • Supplier takes only what's owed -- excess can be retrieved
  • Larger Dead Drop orders build relationship faster than many small ones

Suppliers vs Shop Owners

Note that Dan's Hardware is not a supplier -- it's a shop. The NPC behind the counter is Dan Samwell, a Shop Owner who sells equipment (mixing stations, shelves, etc.) directly for cash. Shop Owners don't use Dead Drops or have relationship bars; they're straightforward merchants.

All 6 Dealers: Best Customer Assignments for Max Profit

Jun 9, 2026
Short answer: There are 6 dealers in Schedule 1: Benji Coleman, Molly Presley, Brad Crosby, Jane Lucero, Wei Long, and Leo Rivers. Each requires a buy-in ($500-$5,000) and unlocks at specific rank thresholds. Assign 8-10 customers per dealer. Prioritize low-spending customers for dealers -- keep your high-spending customers for direct sales. Since v0.4.3, dealers can serve 10 customers each.

The key insight: assign LOW-SPENDING customers to dealers. High-spenders ($200-$500 per visit) should be sold to directly -- the dealer's 20% cut eats too much margin on big purchases. Customer Assignment Strategy

Each dealer can serve up to 10 customers (since v0.4.3; 8 previously). Assigning the right customer types is more important than total count.

PriorityCustomer TypeSpend Per VisitBest For
1 (give to dealer)Low spender$20-$80High frequency, multiple buys per day
2 (give to dealer)Bulk buyer$500+ weeklyClears dealer inventory fast
3 (sell direct)Medium spender$80-$200Daily income, keep margin
4 (sell direct)High spender$200-$500Biggest margin, sell direct

The 60/40 rule: Fill 60% of dealer slots with low-spenders + bulk buyers, and 40% with medium-spenders. Keep all high-spenders for yourself. A dealer with 8 active customers can generate $800-$1,500 per in-game day.

Dealer Management Tips

Inventory check: Open your Dealer Management app every in-game day. If a dealer has held the same product for 2+ days, reassign customers or swap their product. Stale inventory = lost money.

Geographic efficiency: Assign customers near each dealer's location. A dealer in Northtown with Downtown customers wastes travel time. Match customers by district for faster sales cycles.

Product specialization: Each dealer sells slightly faster with their matched product (listed in the table above). Don't switch products unless the dealer is clearly underperforming.

Passive income scaling:

  • 1 dealer active: ~$800-$1,200/day
  • 3 dealers active: ~$3,000-$5,000/day
  • All 6 dealers active: ~$8,000-$15,000/day (with v0.4.3 10-customer cap)

When to Stop Using Dealers

Dealers are optimal for mid-game passive income. Two scenarios where direct sales beat dealers:

  1. High-margin products: Premium cocaine or meth mixes sell for $500-$800 per unit. The dealer's 20% cut loses you $100-$160 per unit on these. Sell high-end products directly.
  2. Post-automation: Once your Barn/Warehouse is fully automated, production outpaces what 6 dealers can move. Use dealers only for overflow inventory at this stage.
