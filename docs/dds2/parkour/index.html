DDS2 Parkour Guide: Movement Mechanics and Police Escape

Jun 9, 2026
The DDS2 Fandom tutorial page confirms: "Use parkour to find shortcuts, to make it easier to escape from enemies chasing you, or to sneak into otherwise inaccessible areas." Parkour is your primary survival tool -- not guns.

The Fandom wiki confirms these moves are specifically designed for:

  • Finding shortcuts between sectors
  • Escaping police and enemy chases
  • Reaching otherwise inaccessible areas

The Steam quick-start guide notes that "Police hours bring extra cash." The corollary: getting caught during police hours costs more than just money -- it raises your wanted level and increases raid probability. Parkour escape is the safest option.

DDS2 Police Evasion: Parkour Escape Routes and Heat Management

Jun 9, 2026
Police in DDS2 have a heat system similar to open-world crime games. Your wanted level (None -> Person of Interest -> Suspect -> Wanted) determines pursuit intensity. Parkour breaks line of sight faster than any other method.

Wanted Level System

Based on the DDS2 Fandom hideout page and community discussions:

LevelNamePolice BehaviorHow to Reduce
0NoneStandard patrolsNo action needed
1Person of InterestIncreased patrols in area3-5 min inactivity
2SuspectActive search, roadblocks5-10 min, avoid crime
3WantedPursuit, helicopter10+ min, hide in safe spot

Parkour Escape Flow

When police start chasing:

  1. Sprint to nearest obstacle (fence, wall, barrier)
  2. Vault or wall-run to break initial line of sight
  3. Climb to higher ground (roof, balcony, ledge)
  4. Crouch and wait -- NPCs cannot path onto most roofs
  5. Once heat drops one level, move to a different sector via parkour shortcuts

The Steam sector connection guide by a community member documents all routes between areas, including police checkpoint locations to avoid.
