{{< callout "info" >}}
The DDS2 tutorial covers basic controls but skips the survival mechanics that experienced players rely on. These 10 tips come from cross-referencing Steam community guides, Reddit discussions, and TheGamer's beginner guide -- all verified through Google search.

{{< /callout >}}

{{< section "Survival Tips" >}}

{{< stepgroup >}}

{{< step num="1" name="Use Multiple Hideouts Early" >}}
<p class="step-desc">Hideout raid risk is affected by wanted level and visibility. The DDS2 Fandom wiki confirms that during a raid, helicopters may appear and you can lose stored assets. <strong>Community strategy</strong>: split raw materials, finished product, and cash across 3+ hideouts. Never keep everything in one location.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="2" name="Parkour Is Your Primary Escape Tool" >}}
<p class="step-desc">The Fandom wiki states: <em>Use parkour to find shortcuts, to make it easier to escape from enemies chasing you, or to sneak into otherwise inaccessible areas.</em> Vaulting, wall-running, and climbing break NPC line of sight instantly -- no weapons needed.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="3" name="Police Heat Affects Raid Probability" >}}
<p class="step-desc">Your wanted level directly impacts how likely your hideouts are to be raided. Heat levels escalate from None to Person of Interest to Suspect to Wanted. Keep heat low by: avoiding gunfire in public, rotating selling locations, and laying low during increased patrol periods.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< /stepgroup >}}

{{< section "Economy and Progression" >}}

{{< stepgroup >}}

{{< step num="4" name="Two-Currency Economy" badge="PRO TIP" type="milestone" >}}
<p class="step-desc">DDS2 uses local currency and USD. The Steam page confirms this dual-currency system as a core feature. <strong>Buy supplies in local currency, sell finished product in USD</strong> -- this exchange differential is where the real profit margin lives.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="5" name="Recipe Cards Are Physical Items" >}}
<p class="step-desc">Recipes are not auto-unlocked. You find recipe cards in the world as collectible items. The first basic meth recipe is in the tutorial zone warehouse. Always explore new areas thoroughly and check every shelf, desk, and container.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="6" name="Use Police Hours for Extra Cash" >}}
<p class="step-desc">A Steam community quick-start guide mentions that police hours bring higher payouts. During peak patrol times, demand increases and customers pay premium prices. Time your production cycles to finish batches just before these windows.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< /stepgroup >}}

{{< section "Staff and Co-op" >}}

{{< stepgroup >}}

{{< step num="7" name="The Distributor Desk Unlocks Staff" badge="PRIORITY" >}}
<p class="step-desc">Before you can assign dealers to handle customers, you need to build a distributor desk at your hideout. This is the critical bottleneck -- until you build it, your employees cannot sell product automatically. Make this your first major purchase after the basic lab kit.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="8" name="Hire Staff Through Multiple Channels" >}}
<p class="step-desc">According to Steam community discussions, you can get employees from three sources: (1) notices on bulletin boards found in town, (2) completing missions for influencers unlocks staff as rewards, and (3) story progression reveals higher-rarity employees with better stats.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="9" name="Co-op Shares the Workload" >}}
<p class="step-desc">DDS2 supports 3-person co-op. Players can share tasks efficiently: one cooks and manages production, one handles deliveries and street sales, one manages staff assignments. This triples your effective throughput per hour compared to solo play.</p>
{{< /step >}}

{{< step num="10" name="Street Cred Unlocks Everything" badge="KEY MILESTONE" type="milestone" >}}
<p class="step-desc"><em>I Am The One Who Knocks</em> (achievement: Street Cred level 4) is the key early milestone. Higher Street Cred unlocks: better recipes, bigger hideout upgrades, more staff slots, and higher-value customer tiers. Prioritize activities that build Street Cred over pure cash grinding.</p>
{{< /step >}}
