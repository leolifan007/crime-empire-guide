{{< callout "info" >}}
Crime Simulator's weapon crafting system lets you upgrade any weapon through 5 tiers. A max-tier weapon deals 3x base damage with 2x range. But the upgrade materials are rare and expensive. Here is the optimal upgrade path for each weapon type.

{{< /callout >}}

{{< section "Weapon Upgrade Tiers" >}}

Every weapon has 5 upgrade slots. Each slot improves a specific stat:

| Slot | Stat Improved | Max Bonus | Materials Needed |
|------|--------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Barrel | Range | +50% | 3x Steel Pipe + 1x Precision Tool |
| Grip | Accuracy | +30% | 2x Rubber + 1x Screw Set |
| Sight | Target Acquisition | +40% | 1x Scope + 2x Glass |
| Magazine | Reload Speed | +40% | 2x Spring + 1x Metal Sheet |
| Stock | Recoil Control | +35% | 2x Wood + 1x Screw Set |

**Total max upgrade:** 2.5x to 3x effective damage (depending on weapon platform).

{{< insight >}}Upgrade the Barrel first on all weapons. Range is the most impactful stat because it lets you engage from beyond NPC detection range (30m), meaning your target never knows where the shot came from.{{< /insight >}}

{{< section "Upgrade Cost Breakdown" >}}

| Upgrade Tier | Materials Cost | Labor Time | Skill Requirement |
|-------------|---------------|-----------|-------------------|
| Tier 1 | $200 | 5 min | None |
| Tier 2 | $500 | 10 min | Crafting 1 |
| Tier 3 | $1,200 | 20 min | Crafting 2 |
| Tier 4 | $3,000 | 40 min | Crafting 3 |
| Tier 5 | $8,000 | 60 min | Crafting 5 |

**Total cost for a full upgrade:** ~$12,900 in materials. vs buying a Tier 5 weapon from the black market ($35,000). Upgrading saves 63% but costs 135 minutes of workbench time.

| Weapon | Full Upgrade Cost | Black Market Price | Savings |
|--------|------------------|-------------------|---------|
| Pistol | $12,900 | $22,000 | 41% |
| SMG | $12,900 | $35,000 | 63% |
| Sniper | $12,900 | $50,000 | 74% |
| Bat (melee) | $8,000 | N/A (cannot buy) | N/A |
| Knife | $8,000 | N/A | N/A |

{{< section "Material Farming Routes" >}}

The bottleneck is Precision Tools and Scopes. These cannot be crafted -- only found or bought.

| Material | Buy Price | Best Farming Location | Drop Rate |
|----------|----------|----------------------|-----------|
| Steel Pipe | $50/hardware store | Construction sites | 3-5 per site |
| Rubber | $30/hardware store | Garages, auto shops | 2-4 per location |
| Screw Set | $40/hardware store | Hardware stores, toolboxes | 2-3 per location |
| Glass | $25/hardware store | Electronics stores, labs | 2-4 per location |
| Precision Tool | Not buyable | Warehouse B safe (guaranteed), police station (rare) | 1 per 3-4 runs |
| Scope | Not buyable | Gun shop back room, police armory | 1 per 5 runs |

{{< callout "warning" >}}Precision Tools and Scopes are the gatekeeper materials. You will upgrade 3-4 weapons before you find your first scope. Prioritize them in your loot runs -- they are worth more as upgrade materials than their $250 fence price.{{< /callover >}}

{{< section "Weapon-Specific Upgrade Priorities" >}}

### Silenced Pistol (Best All-Rounder)

| Upgrade Order | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| 1. Barrel | 30m base -> 45m (outranges all NPC detection) |
| 2. Magazine | 2s reload -> 1.2s reload (faster follow-up shots) |
| 3. Grip | 30% accuracy (makes 45m shots reliable) |
| 4. Stock | Recoil control for rapid fire |
| 5. Sight | Least impactful on pistol platform |

### Hunting Rifle (Best Sniper Option)

| Upgrade Order | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| 1. Barrel | 100m -> 150m (half the map in range) |
| 2. Sight | 40% target acquisition (makes 150m shots feasible) |
| 3. Stock | Recoil for follow-up shots |
| 4. Grip | Marginal improvement |
| 5. Magazine | Rarely need more than 1 shot on a sniper |

### Bat (Best Melee Starter)

| Upgrade Order | Why |
|---------------|-----|
| 1. Grip | 30% accuracy (increases hitbox, fewer misses) |
| 2. Barrel | Only adds 1m range, but 1m matters in melee |
| 3. Stock | Swing speed |
| 4. Sight | Useless on melee (skip entirely) |
| 5. Magazine | Useless on melee (skip entirely) |

{{< section "Workbench Location" >}}

You need access to a workbench to craft. There are 4 in the game:

| Location | Tier Available | Access | Notes |
|----------|---------------|--------|-------|
| Hideout (starter) | 1-2 | Free | Basic crafting only |
| Warehouse B | 1-4 | Purchase $5,000 | Best mid-game bench |
| Underground Bunker | 1-5 | Complete side quest | Best bench, rarest |
| Police Station Armory | 1-3 | Heist access | Not recommended |

**Optimal workbench:** Upgrade to Warehouse B bench as soon as possible. It handles 90% of your crafting needs. The Underground Bunker is needed only for Tier 5 upgrades.
