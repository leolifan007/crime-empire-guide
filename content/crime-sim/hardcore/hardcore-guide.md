{{< callout "info" >}}
Hardcore Mode in Crime Simulator removes autosaves, doubles police response speed, triples guard count, and applies permanent death. One mistake ends your run. This guide covers the strategies that keep hardcore runs alive to endgame.

{{< /callout >}}

{{< section "What Changes in Hardcore Mode" >}}

| Factor | Normal Mode | Hardcore Mode |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| Save system | Manual + autosave | Manual only (sleep at hideout) |
| Death penalty | Respawn at hospital | Permanent -- save deleted |
| Police heat decay | 3 min per level | 10 min per level |
| Guard density | 1-3 per location | 3-8 per location |
| NPC damage | 1x | 2x (one-shot potential) |
| Alarm response | 30 seconds | 15 seconds |
| Bribe cost | $500-1,000 | $2,000-5,000 |
| Fence prices | 100% | 70% |
| Lockpick difficulty | Normal | +1 tier on all locks |

{{< callout "warning" >}}Hardcore mode is not "Normal mode but harder." It is a fundamentally different game. Strategies that work in Normal (aggressive play, trial-and-error lockpicking, multiple alarms) will kill you in Hardcore. Assume every wrong move is your last.{{< /callout >}}

{{< section "Hardcore Skill Priority" >}}

Skills matter 5x more in Hardcore because you cannot afford mistakes.

| Priority | Skill | Why |
|----------|-------|-----|
| 1 | Lockpicking 3 | Failed lockpick = alarm = death |
| 2 | Stealth Movement 3 | Detection = fight = likely death |
| 3 | Driving 2 | Fast extraction = survival |
| 4 | Combatives 3 | When stealth fails, you need to win fast |
| 5 | Pickpocketing 2 | Keys and intel without breaking entry |

**Skill point investment order (first 15 points):**

1. Lockpicking 1 (1pt)
2. Stealth Movement 1 (2pts)
3. Lockpicking 2 (3pts)
4. Stealth Movement 2 (3pts)
5. Lockpicking 3 (5pts)
6. Driving 1 (1pt)

Total: 15 skill points = 15 jobs completed before tackling any medium-risk missions.

{{< section "Hardcore Mission Selection" >}}

| Risk Level | Job Types | Hardcore Recommendation |
|-----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Very Low | Loot Vehicle, Vandalism | Safe for first 10 jobs |
| Low | Steal Item (suburban) | Acceptable with Stealth 1+ |
| Medium | Break In, Steal Document | Only with Lockpicking 2+ |
| High | Debt Collection, Eliminate Target | Avoid until Lockpicking 3 + Stealth 2 |
| Very High | Heists | Only with full gear + team |

{{< insight >}}In Hardcore mode, your survival rate per job is the product of each risk factor. A Steal Item job with Lockpicking 1 (80% success), no stealth skill (70% detection avoidance), and no escape plan (60% survival) = 0.8 x 0.7 x 0.6 = 33.6% survival per job. By job 10, your cumulative survival probability is 0.336^10 = 0.002%. In Hardcore, invest in survival skills before anything else.{{< /insight >}}

{{< section "Hardcore-Specific Strategies" >}}

### The 3-Hideout Rule

You need 3 hideouts before doing any high-value job in Hardcore:

1. **Primary hideout** -- Sleep/save here, $5k max stored
2. **Stash hideout** -- All loot stored here (never visit after saving)
3. **Emergency hideout** -- Unknown to police, $1k bribe cash + basic gear

If your primary is compromised, you still have the stash. If the stash is found, you still have emergency gear.

### The 30-Second Rule

If any phase of a job takes longer than 30 seconds from your expectation, abort. The game gives you one tell before a complication occurs: NPC route deviation, camera flicker, door being locked when expected unlocked. Any deviation = abort immediately.

### No-Fight Policy

In Hardcore mode, do not fight unless cornered. Fighting:

- Draws attention (more NPCs arrive within 20s)
- Costs HP (no hospitals, medkits heal 50% with 5m animation)
- Risks weapon drop (10% chance per combat encounter)
- Creates witness (permanent heat in district)

**Escape priority:** Smoke bomb > Flashbang > Sprint > Hide > Bribe > Fight
