{{< callout "info" >}}
**Short answer:** Crime Simulator drops you into a open-world criminal sandstorm with no handholding. Your first hour is about understanding the job board system, buying basic tools, and completing your first 3 jobs without triggering a police response.

{{< /callout >}}

{{< section "Starting Out: What to Do First" >}}

You start in a rundown apartment with $200, a basic lockpick, and a phone. Skip the tutorial popups and do this sequence:

1. **Open the Job Board app** on your phone
2. **Accept any "Steal Item" job** (marked with a box icon) -- these are the easiest
3. **Buy a crowbar** from the hardware store ($50) -- you need it for entry
4. **Travel to the job location** (follow the GPS marker)
5. **Complete the job** (enter building, find item, escape)

Each job pays $200-500. Do 3-4 jobs to build your first $1,000.

{{< insight >}}Do not accept "Elimination" or "Debt Collection" jobs in your first hour. They require combat skills and weapons you do not have. Stick to "Steal Item" and "Break In" jobs until you have $2,000 in the bank.{{< /insight >}}

{{< section "Core Mechanics Explained" >}}

### Heat System

| Level | Name | Effect | Clean Time |
|-------|------|--------|------------|
| 0 | Clean | No suspicion | - |
| 1 | Mild | Police alerts triggered on line of sight | 3 min |
| 2 | Warm | Active search in district | 8 min |
| 3 | Hot | Roadblocks, helicopter | 15 min |
| 4 | Burning | Full lockdown | 30 min |

**Heat reduction:** Go to any hideout and wait. Do not commit crimes while heat is above level 1. The game tracks your recent activity and applies multipliers.

### Skill Points

You earn 1 skill point per job completed. Spend them in the Skills tab:

| Skill | Cost | Effect |
|-------|------|--------|
| Lockpicking Lv 1 | 1 pt | Open basic locks |
| Lockpicking Lv 2 | 3 pts | Open medium locks |
| Lockpicking Lv 3 | 5 pts | Open any lock |
| Stealth Movement | 2 pts | 30% quieter footsteps |
| Pickpocketing | 2 pts | Steal from NPCs without detection |
| Driving | 1 pt | 15% faster car handling |
| Combatives | 2 pts | +25% melee damage |

**Optimal first 5 skill points:** Lockpicking 1 (1pt) > Stealth Movement (2pts) > Lockpicking 2 (3pts > remaining). This gives you access to medium difficulty jobs which pay 2x more.

{{< section "The Tool Kit" >}}

| Tool | Cost | Use | Priority |
|------|------|-----|----------|
| Crowbar | $50 | Forced entry (windows, weak doors) | 1st purchase |
| Basic Lockpick | $100 | Opens 1-2 pin locks | 2nd purchase |
| Flashlight | $30 | Dark buildings | 3rd purchase |
| Duct Tape | $20 | Muffle sound on broken windows | Optional |
| Sleeping Gas | $200 | Disable 1 NPC for 60 seconds | After first $2k |
| Bolt Cutters | $150 | Cut chains and padlocks | After first $2k |

{{< section "First 5 Jobs Strategy" >}}

| Job # | Type | Payout | Location | Strategy |
|-------|------|--------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Steal Item | $200 | Suburban house | Enter through back window, grab item, exit same way |
| 2 | Steal Item | $250 | Small office | Nighttime, use crowbar on side door |
| 3 | Break In | $300 | Warehouse | Use lockpick on rear entrance, hide if guard patrols |
| 4 | Steal Document | $350 | Bank back office | Riskier, requires timing guard rotations |
| 5 | Loot Vehicle | $200 | Parked cars | Nighttime, quiet streets, smash window and grab |

After job 5, you should have ~$1,500. At this point, buy a lockpick kit and upgrade your crowbar to a Tier 2 model ($120).

{{< section "Common Beginner Mistakes" >}}

| Mistake | Consequence | Fix |
|---------|------------|-----|
| Running near job site | Heat increase before starting | Walk within 50m of target |
| Breaking front windows | Alarm triggers | Use side/rear entry always |
| Fighting instead of hiding | Unnecessary health loss | Flee if spotted, fight only if cornered |
| Storing loot at apartment | Raid target | Buy second stash location ($500 from pawn shop) |
| Ignoring time of day | 3x more witnesses during day | Plan jobs for 22:00-04:00 |
